What is Ready to Work?

Ready to Work is an educational and job placement program designed to help thousandsof San Antonians improve their quality of life.

How can we work for you?


  • Finding the education and resources that will get you the job that you want
  • Paying all the tuition for the education path that you choose
  • Providing emergency funds to help overcome any barriers to your future
  • Connecting you to local nonprofits to help with child care, dental care, and
    any obstacles that may appear
  • Connecting you with employers across San Antonio
  • Helping to land you a high paying job with room for growth and promotion

Are you eligible?


  • 18 years old when applying for the program
  • Permitted to work in the United States
  • Resident of the City of San Antonio
  • Income less than 250% of federal poverty guidelines ($33,975 for an individual, or $69,375 for a household of four)
  • Not currently enrolled in college courses at intake
  • Willing to take assessments relating to barriers, academics, skills aptitude, and workplace readiness
  • Committed to completing the program

Frequently Asked Questions


Q: How long will it take to get started?

A: After you enroll, our case managers will have one week to contact you. From then, you’ll work with the case manager to get enrolled in your educational program within one month.

Q: How do I know that I’ll get a good job after I finish my training?

A: We have partnered with over 200 employers across San Antonio to make sure that you’re getting education for jobs that are in demand and jobs that are paying at least $15 an hour. The list of employers can be found here.

Q: Will I get paid to go to school?

A: No. Ready to Work will provide money for tuition and emergency funding, but we cannot pay you to go to school.

Q: What kind of training do you provide?

A: We offer a variety of training programs from high-school equivalencies, to certifications, to associates degrees, to bachelors degrees across a variety of sectors. Our full list of programs can be accessed here.

Q: What if I don’t have enough money for transportation/child care/internet access/housing/food/utilities?

A: We have emergency funds to put towards each participant to help remove any obstacles that may get in the way of your education.
